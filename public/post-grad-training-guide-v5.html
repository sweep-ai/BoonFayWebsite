Section 01

Why You Fell Off After Graduation

Nobody warns you about this part. You graduated, got the job, figured out the apartment, and somewhere in the middle of all of it your body quietly started changing in ways you didn't want. The workouts got less frequent. The energy disappeared somewhere between your morning commute and your third meeting of the day. And before you knew it months had passed without a single consistent week in the gym.

This isn't a motivation problem. It's a structure problem. College gave you a built in schedule, a gym ten minutes from your dorm, and enough rhythm that even your laziest weeks had something holding them together. Post grad life strips all of that away overnight. Nothing external is forcing your healthy habits to happen anymore and without a system to replace that structure most guys just drift. For months. Sometimes years. This guide gives you the system.

Section 02

The Only Thing That Actually Matters

Consistency. Everything else is just detail built on top of it. Three to four days a week in the gym, every single week, no matter how the workday went. Not perfect sessions. Not peak motivation. Just showing up and doing the work. The guy who does that for a full year will always look better than the guy who goes hard for two weeks, burns out, and starts over every few months.

While you are in there, try to do a little more than you did last session. More weight, one extra rep, better control. This is called progressive overload and it is the only mechanism that actually changes your body. Your muscles grow when they are forced to do more than they are used to. Track your lifts every session and show up the next time trying to beat what you did. That is really the whole formula.

⚡
The rule that changes everything: Never miss two sessions in a row. Missing one is just a rest day. Missing two in a row is the start of a broken habit. If you miss a session the next one is non negotiable. No guilt, no waiting until Monday. You just go.
Section 03

Train Before Work

Every session you finish before 8am is a session nothing can cancel. The unexpected meeting, the coworker asking you to grab a drink, the exhaustion that hits around 6pm. None of it can touch a workout you already finished. This is the single most effective scheduling decision you can make as a post grad man and the difference between the guys who train consistently and the guys who just intend to.

Morning training also makes you measurably better at your job. Exercise releases brain chemicals that sharpen your focus, memory, and mood for hours afterward. You are not sacrificing career time for gym time. You are investing in the quality of every hour that follows.

Section 04

The Program That Fits Your Life

The best program is the one you can actually show up to consistently around a full time job. If you are new or getting back into it after a long break start with three full body sessions per week. Once early progress slows move to the upper lower split four days a week. Whatever you choose run it for at least twelve weeks before switching anything because results take eight to twelve weeks to actually show up and most guys quit right before they would have seen them.

💪
Beginner: 0 to 6 Months
Full Body 3x Per Week

Monday, Wednesday, Friday. Alternate Workout A (Squat, Bench, Row) and Workout B (Squat, Overhead Press, Deadlift). Add 5 pounds to upper body lifts and 10 pounds to lower body lifts every single session.

Section 05

Training for Your Goal

The program stays the same no matter what your goal is. What changes is how you eat around it. Pick where you are right now and run it.

🏗️
Build Muscle
+200 to 300 CALS
Eat slightly above maintenance and keep most sets in the 6 to 12 rep range. Hit your bodyweight in pounds as grams of protein every day. You cannot build muscle without enough protein no matter how hard you train.
🔥
Lose Fat
300 to 500 CALS BELOW
Eat below maintenance and keep lifting just as heavy as you always have. This is where most guys go wrong. Heavy lifting tells your body to hold onto muscle while the fat drops. Add two to three cardio sessions and keep everything else identical.
⚡
Both at Once
MAINTENANCE
Eat at maintenance with one gram of protein per pound of bodyweight and focus on getting stronger every session. Progress is slower but you never look worse in the process. For most post grad men just getting serious this is the best place to start.
⚠️
The most common mistake: Eating in a deficit and switching to light weight high rep training. Your body ends up losing muscle and fat together so you get smaller but still soft. Keep lifting heavy no matter what your goal is.
Section 06

Cardio Without Overthinking It

Two to three sessions of 20 to 40 minutes alongside your lifting is enough when your goal is building muscle. Bump it to three to four sessions when cutting. The best pace is one where you could hold a conversation but it takes a little effort. It burns fat, helps your body recover between lifting sessions, and doesn't wreck your energy for work or training. Never do intense cardio right before you lift. A ten minute warmup is fine. A 45 minute run before squatting is not.

🚶
Best Default
Brisk Walk
Easiest to recover from. Burns fat without touching your recovery between lifting sessions. Perfect for rest days when you want to move without stressing your body.
🚣
Most Underused
Rowing Machine
Works your legs, back, arms, and core all at the same time. Twenty minutes on the rower is a genuinely complete session. If you hate cardio try this before you make up your mind.
🚴
Good Option
Bike or Light Jog
Easy on your joints and easy to keep intensity dialed in. Great if you need something low effort that still gets the blood moving.
Section 07

Recovery Is Part of the Program

Your muscles don't grow in the gym. They grow while you rest. Two to three rest days per week isn't laziness. It is the actual mechanism of progress. Most guys treat recovery like an afterthought and then wonder why they feel beat up and stuck. Treat it as seriously as you treat the sessions themselves.

🥩
Eat After Every Session
Get protein and carbs in within 45 to 60 minutes of finishing. Chicken and rice, a protein shake with a banana, Greek yogurt and granola. Use the window every single session without exception.
😴
Sleep 7 to 8 Hours
This is literally where muscle gets built. You cannot supplement or train your way around chronic sleep deprivation. It is the most powerful recovery tool you have and it costs nothing.
🚶
Move on Rest Days
A walk or some light stretching on your off days is perfect especially when your job already has you sitting at a desk for eight hours. Just move without stressing your body.
6 to 8
Weeks
Take a Deload Week

Every six to eight weeks drop to about sixty percent of your normal weights for one full week. Same movements, just lighter. Your joints, tendons, and nervous system get to genuinely recover and you will come back noticeably stronger. Almost no post grad men do this. All of them should.

Section 08

When Life Gets in the Way

There will be brutal work weeks. There will be mornings where the alarm goes off and getting up feels genuinely impossible. This is just post grad life and it doesn't mean your routine is broken. The ability to stay consistent through the messy imperfect weeks is exactly what separates the guys who actually transform their bodies from the ones who keep starting over.

01
Never Miss Two in a Row

Missing one session is just a rest day. Missing two in a row is the beginning of a broken habit. If you miss, the next session is non negotiable. No guilt, no waiting until Monday. You just go.

02
Lower the Bar on Your Worst Days

Go in, do your three main compound movements, and leave. A 25 minute session beats nothing every single time. And almost every time you get yourself through the door for 25 minutes you end up doing the full workout anyway. The hardest part is always just walking in.

03
Train Regardless of Where You Are

When work travel comes up find the hotel gym and use it. Bodyweight work and dumbbells is more than enough to maintain your conditioning for a week. The habit of training no matter where you are is one of the most valuable ones you can build.

Section 09

The Four Things That Move the Needle

Everything else in this guide is just detail built on top of these four. Get these right and the rest follows on its own.

📅
01 · Show Up
Consistency Every Week

Three to four sessions per week, every week, no matter what. This is the whole game. Every other variable, the program, nutrition, supplements, is just optimizing on top of this one thing. Get this right first.

📈
02 · Get Stronger
Progressive Overload Every Session

Add weight or reps to your main lifts consistently. This is the only mechanism of muscle growth. Without it nothing else matters. Track every session and show up the next time trying to beat what you did.

🥩
03 · Hit Protein
Your Bodyweight in Grams Daily

Your bodyweight in pounds equals your grams of protein per day. Do it through real food and use a protein shake to close the gap when you need to. You cannot build muscle without it no matter how hard you train.

🌙
04 · Sleep
Seven to Eight Hours Every Night

This is where your body actually builds the muscle your training signals it to build. You cannot supplement or train your way around chronic sleep deprivation. It is the most powerful recovery tool you have and it is completely free.
